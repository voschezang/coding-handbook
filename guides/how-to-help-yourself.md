# Self Help

All self help books repeat these ideas. A lot of them originate from Buddhism.

1. Take **responsibility** for your own life. Suffering is inevitable, but your response is your freedom.
2. Create good **habits**. Routines make things effortless. Consistency > intensity.
3. **Clarity** brings alignment.
4. **Connection** is built on presence and empathy.
5. Self-**discipline** is the willingness to be uncomfortable. Investment implies discomfort. Good strategy means saying no.
6. Don’t control **emotions** and don’t let emotions control you. Feel emotions > fuel them. Emotions are addictive.
7. Slow down to speed up. Being busy a prioritization problem, not a time problem. Impatience ≠ urgency
8. All models are wrong. 
9. Planning > plans. No plan survives first contact.

Work-releated principles.

1. Go fast but don’t hurry. Deliver sooner, rather than faster. Move fast AND with small steps. Obstacles are the way.
2. Minimize work to maximize **outcomes**. Outcome > output. Activity ≠ achievement.
